01

Category positioning

Helped create the Product Marketing Environment category

From generic AI to a distinct system

The challenge

Aisepedia risked looking like another AI content generator in a crowded market. The product needed a category buyers could understand and a reason to choose it over a general-purpose model.

What I did

  1. DefinedHelped define and operationalize Product Marketing Environment as the product category.
  2. BuiltBuilt the story from market research and ICP selection through positioning, messaging, and execution.
  3. ContrastedContrasted connected PMM memory, frameworks, and system sync with one-off AI outputs.
  4. CenteredKept the product marketer as the central actor and Aisepedia as the environment supporting better decisions.

Messaging shipped

“The first Product Marketing Environment.”
“You update the product. We handle the ripple.”
“Aisepedia isn’t the hero of the story. You are.”

32 discovery sessions supplied the buyer language behind the category narrative.

27 design partners tested the product story and challenged its assumptions.

$400K in qualified pipeline across 134 tracked accounts came from the broader go-to-market motion.

Helped land the first enterprise procurement deal with Splunk (Cisco) via a design partner.

02

Homepage and use cases

Turned customer research into audience-specific messaging

From broad promise to four buyer problems

The challenge

“AI for product marketers” was too broad to carry the product. Different product marketing roles needed to recognize their own operational problem and see how the same connected workflow addressed it.

What I did

I synthesized design-partner conversations into four audiences, structured the homepage around outcomes instead of a feature inventory, and threaded customer language through the research-to-execution story.

Solo PMMs

Keep launches, messaging, and competitive intelligence out of one person’s head.

PMM teams

Align research, positioning, and messaging without duplicating work.

Heads of PMM

Standardize execution and preserve clarity as the team scales.

Consultants

Deliver structured client strategy without rebuilding the process each time.

03

Customer evidence

Built proof for a category with no inherited credibility

From product claims to practitioner validation

The challenge

A new category has no inherited credibility. Product claims needed direct evidence from practitioners who could test ICP precision, differentiated messaging, research depth, and cross-artifact consistency.

What I did

  1. BuiltBuilt the design-partner motion around “Built with you, not just for you.”
  2. ConvertedConverted feedback into named, attributable testimonials tied to specific product claims.
  3. ActivatedMoved selected partners into public proof, panels, community programming, and customer conversations.

27 design partners formed the customer-evidence base.

18 senior participants held Director, VP, or SVP-level roles.

32 research sessions became product, messaging, and roadmap inputs.

4 senior practitioners carried the category conversation into the PMM 2.0 panel.

04

Activation and lifecycle

Extended messaging into the product and measured it

Direct measured result

The challenge

Users were abandoning the experience before reaching meaningful value. The team needed to know where they stopped, what message would move them forward, and which product intervention belonged with it.

What I did

  1. DefinedDefined an eight-stage conversion funnel and 11 acquisition and activation events.
  2. BuiltBuilt five demo, onboarding, and re-engagement touches around observed behavior.
  3. MappedMapped 12 abandonment stages to targeted messages and product interventions.
  4. IteratedUsed drop-off data to inform onboarding changes and recovery priorities.
24%
reduction in early-stage abandonment

Result of analytics, lifecycle messaging, and onboarding changes working together.

11 tracked events covered acquisition and activation behavior.

22 lifecycle KPIs defined progress and warning signals.

5 lifecycle touches supported demos, onboarding, and re-engagement.

12 recovery stages paired user behavior with a specific response.
